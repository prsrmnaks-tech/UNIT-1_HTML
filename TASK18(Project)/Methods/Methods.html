Natural Farming Methods

Traditional farming methods using natural inputs

Natural farming methods are agricultural practices that improve crop production without using chemical fertilizers or pesticides. These methods focus on soil health, biodiversity, and environmental balance.

Common Techniques

  • 1.crop rotation: Growing different types of crops in the same area across seasons to improve soil fertility and reduce pest buildup.
  • 2.composting: Decomposing organic matter like plant residues and animal waste to create nutrient-rich compost for soil amendment.
  • 3.green manure: Growing specific cover crops that are plowed back into the soil to enhance its nutrient content.
  • 4.biological pest control: Using natural predators or beneficial insects to manage pest populations instead of chemical pesticides.

chemical fertilizers, if used, should be applied in the recommended quantity to avoid damage to crops and soil. Fertilizers can be applied by broadcasting over the field, placing near the plant base, or dissolving in water and spraying on crops. Fertilizer application should be done during suitable weather conditions, preferably before irrigation or rainfall. Proper and balanced use of fertilizers improves crop yield, maintains soil health, and protects the environment.

How to use Fertilizers

Fertilizers should be used carefully to support healthy plant growth and soil fertility. Before applying fertilizers, the soil condition and crop type should be understood. Organic or natural fertilizers like compost, cow dung, or vermicompost should be well-decomposed before use. These fertilizers are mixed into the soil near the plant roots to provide nutrients slowly.
