Why Natural Farming is Important?

Natural farming plays a major role in protecting soil health and maintaining environmental balance. It avoids the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, allowing beneficial microorganisms to thrive in the soil. This method improves soil structure, increases water retention, and reduces land degradation over time. Natural farming supports biodiversity by encouraging insects, birds, and earthworms that naturally control pests. It also helps farmers reduce cultivation costs and minimizes health risks caused by chemical exposure. By producing safe, nutritious, and chemical-free food, natural farming supports sustainable agriculture and ensures long-term food security for future generations.

Difference Between Chemical and Natural Farming

Chemical farming depends heavily on synthetic fertilizers and pesticides to increase crop yield, but it often damages soil health over time. These chemicals kill beneficial microorganisms and pollute water sources through runoff. In contrast, natural farming uses organic inputs like compost, cow dung, and bio-fertilizers to nourish the soil. Natural farming improves soil structure and increases biodiversity. It produces healthier and chemical-free food for consumers. Overall, natural farming supports long-term sustainability while chemical farming focuses on short-term productivity.

Benefits of Natural Farming

Natural farming reduces farming costs because farmers depend less on expensive chemical inputs. It improves farmer health by reducing exposure to toxic chemicals and ensures safe food for consumers.

Environmentally, it helps restore soil fertility, encourages beneficial insects, and reduces climate impact by improving carbon storage in soil.
